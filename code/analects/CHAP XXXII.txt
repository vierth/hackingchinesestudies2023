1. The Master said, 'When a man's knowledge issufficient to attain, and his virtue is not sufficient to enable him tohold, whatever he may have gained, he will lose again. 2. 'When his knowledge is sufficient to attain, and he hasvirtue enough to hold fast, if he cannot govern with dignity, thepeople will not respect him. 3. 'When his knowledge is sufficient to attain, and he hasvirtue enough to hold fast; when he governs also with dignity, yet ifhe try to move the people contrary to the rules of propriety:-- fullexcellence is not reached.'
