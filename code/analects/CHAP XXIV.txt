Shu-sun Wu-shu having spoken revilingly ofChung-ni, Tsze-kung said, 'It is of no use doing so. Chung-ni cannotbe reviled. The talents and virtue of other men are hillocks andmounds which may be stepped over. Chung-ni is the sun or moon,which it is not possible to step over. Although a man may wish tocut himself off from the sage, what harm can he do to the sun ormoon? He only shows that he does not know his own capacity.
